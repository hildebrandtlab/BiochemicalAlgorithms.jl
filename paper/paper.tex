\section{Introduction}
Structural Bioinformatics is characterized as a field of research that combines and applies knowledge from diverse disciplines ranging from numerical computation, through fundamental physical laws such as Newton's equation of motion to complex situations requiring in-depth knowledge of biochemistry.  
The aim of this research field is the analysis and manipulation of three dimensional structures of biological macromolecules such as proteins and nucleic acids. Typical applications are found in molecular modelling and the application of molecular mechanic e.g., force fields to compute the energy of thh examining structure. 

Why do we have to run simulations on the computer? Why is it necessary to perform it on a computer? Example Protein Ligand Docking... As an example.
